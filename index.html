Your Health Plan

Evidence-Based
🫁
Priority: Respiratory Stability First
Obesity Hypoventilation Syndrome (OHS) means abdominal fat is compressing your diaphragm, reducing lung volume — especially when lying flat. This must be addressed immediately alongside all other interventions.

🫁 Priority 1 — Fix Breathing (Urgent)

1
Request CPAP Machine
If experiencing nighttime oxygen drops, a CPAP is the single most important step. Better oxygenation = better sleep = better metabolism and energy. Ask your doctor immediately.
2
Pursed Lip Breathing — Morning & Night
Inhale slowly for 2 counts through your nose. Exhale for 4 counts through pursed lips (like blowing through a straw). Do 10 minutes each session. This trains the diaphragm to push against abdominal compression.
3
Elevated Sleep Position
Never sleep flat. Use a wedge pillow or elevate the head of your bed by 30–45°. This single change alone reduces lung compression significantly during sleep and can improve overnight oxygen saturation.

🥗 Priority 2 — Diet Overhaul

1
Protein at Every Meal (Min 80–100g/day)
Eggs, Greek yogurt, lentils, chicken, fish. Protein preserves muscle mass during weight loss and keeps you full longer. This is your most powerful dietary tool.
2
Eliminate Liquid Calories Entirely
No juices, sodas, sweetened chai, or flavoured drinks. Switch to water, black tea, and black coffee. Liquid calories don't trigger satiety signals — they are silent saboteurs.
3
Eat Slowly — Minimum 20 Minutes Per Meal
Satiety signals take 15–20 minutes to reach the brain. Eating fast causes consistent overeating. Put the fork down between bites. Stop at 80% full.
4
Target: 1,400–1,600 kcal/day
A modest deficit without starvation. Cut ultra-processed foods — biscuits, packaged snacks, white bread. These are engineered to override your fullness signals.

🚶 Priority 3 — Movement Within Capacity

1
Seated Exercises (Start Here)
Seated leg raises, seated marching in a chair. Builds circulation and leg muscle without joint stress. Do 10 minutes daily to start — no equipment needed.
2
10-Minute Daily Walk
Even at a slow pace. The goal is not burning calories — it's preventing further deconditioning and improving lung function. Build to 20, then 30 minutes over weeks.
3
Water Walking (If Accessible)
Buoyancy removes 70% of body weight, making movement feasible even now. Water aerobics is the gold standard for mobility-limited patients at this weight.

💊 Priority 4 — Supplements (Discuss With Doctor)

Vitamin D
Nearly universally deficient in obese patients. Critical for metabolism and immunity.
Magnesium
Supports sleep quality, muscle function, and blood sugar regulation.
Omega-3 (Fish Oil)
Reduces systemic inflammation that worsens OHS and metabolic dysfunction.
Vitamin B12
Often depleted. Essential for energy levels and nerve function.

💡
Key Message
Every kilogram lost before surgery reduces surgical risk and improves anesthesia safety. Even a 5–8 kg loss before any procedure makes a significant clinical difference. A 5–10% body weight loss meaningfully reduces cardiovascular and metabolic risk — that's a realistic and achievable goal. Your motivation is your greatest asset right now.
